# Oblique Copy Self-Review Checklist

Run every item against the copy before delivering. Fix failures — don't flag them to Sean.
The goal is that Sean receives copy that already passes, not copy with a list of its own problems.

---

## Structure

- [ ] **Hero passes the zero-context test.** Read the headline knowing nothing about the brand or product. Does it immediately make you feel something or picture an outcome? If you have to think about what it means, rewrite it.
- [ ] **Format matches execution.** Static ad: one punchy outcome line, stops there. Landing page: headline triggers imagination, subheadline earns the product story.
- [ ] **Customer is the hero.** Can the reader see themselves in the outcome? If the opening block is entirely the brand's story with no reader in it, add one outcome-led line.
- [ ] **No hook explanation.** If the opening line is followed immediately by a sentence that confirms or defends it, cut the second sentence.

---

## Language

- [ ] **No em dashes.** Scan the entire copy for " — ". Replace every one with a period, colon, or comma. Rewrite the sentence if none of those work.
- [ ] **No antithetical pivot sentences.** Search for "not just", "isn't just", "more than just", "it's not about X, it's about Y". Replace every instance with a direct claim.
- [ ] **No filler openers.** Check the first sentence of every section. Remove: "In today's world", "Now more than ever", "In a crowded marketplace", "At the end of the day".
- [ ] **No hollow power words.** Remove: game-changer, revolutionary, transformative, disruptive, world-class, cutting-edge, synergy, seamlessly, holistic, bespoke — unless earned and precise.

---

## Argumentation

- [ ] **No double-landing.** Read each paragraph. If two consecutive sentences make the same point in different words, cut one. Argue once. Trust the first landing.
- [ ] **Specific over vague.** Every claim that can be made specific should be. "10 times softer than polyester" beats "exceptionally soft." Numbers, names, outcomes.
- [ ] **Competitor copy-paste test.** Could a competitor paste any sentence into their own deck and have it still make sense? If yes, make it more specific to this brand, product, and moment.

---

## Rhythm

- [ ] **Vary sentence length.** If three sentences in a row are roughly the same length, break the pattern.
- [ ] **Fragments earn their place.** A fragment should land hard. If it doesn't add impact, turn it into a full sentence.

---

## Factual integrity

- [ ] **Flag unverified claims.** Any specific number, guarantee, or superlative that hasn't been confirmed by the client gets noted after the copy for Sean to verify before publishing.
